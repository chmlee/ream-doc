Data language for creating maintainable social science datasets
Why REAM Get Started

NOTE

The project is under active development and is not production ready. You are free to play around with the language and tools, but note that backward compatibility is not guaranteed.

Migrating from the old documentation is a work in progress; information and features may be missing. All contributions are welcome.

REAM is an alternative to spreadsheets and databases for managing social science datasets. It has three main components:

  1. a data serialization language for structured datasets (working in progress)
  2. a data template language to generate datasets (planned)
  3. a collection of filters to manipulate data (planned)

REAM encourages inline documentation through annotations, and compiles to both analysis-ready datasets (CSV, JSON, etc.) and human-readable documentations (HTML, PDF, etc.) Two formats, one source.

The language, along with the toolchain, aims to help social scientists build human-readable, modular and interoperable datasets for social science, ranging from small personal projects to large collaborative projects.
