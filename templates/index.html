Data language for creating maintainable social science datasets
Why REAM Get Started

REAM has three main components:

  1. a data serialization language for structured datasets (working in progress)
  2. a data template language to generate datasets (planned)
  3. a collection of filters to manipulate data (planned)

REAM encourages inline documentation through annotations, and compiles to both analysis-ready datasets (CSV, JSON, etc.) and human-readable documentations (HTML, PDF, etc.) Two formats, one source.

The language, along with the toolchain, aims to create human-readable, modular and interoperable datasets for social science, ranging from small personal projects to large collaborative projects.
